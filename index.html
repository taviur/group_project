Study More

You may be addicted to learning, which is great (you nerd! no really, learning is great) so maybe you're thinking about taking a Masters or a PhD and wondering how the fuck that whole ting even works, because lord knows we had no bloody clue.


First off, we'll define post-study study as 'taught courses' and 'research courses' because as we struggled to find out, not all master programmes are made the same.

Taught Courses


A taught course means, well, you'll be taught more things! You can take a Master of Arts (MA), Master of Science (MSc) or Master of Business Administration (MBA).


Research Courses


A research course means... you'll be researching stuff! You can take a Masters research course or a PhD.

World of Work

Blimey, now this is a hard one...

Whether you want to get onto a graduate scheme, or you want to just jump in and figure out what you actually want to do later - there's definitely going to be something out there for you!

Websites like WikiJobs will help you narrow down the right graduate job for you. Whereas recruitment companies like Reed.co.uk and Indeed will give you a broader look at what's out there and match you with some employers.


Something Else

You may not want to work, or study more, you may be undecided, but that's all cool - you've got plenty of time mate!


Some other things you can do could be: travel the world, start up your own company, take a couple internships, get temporary work, volunteer for something you believe in, become a stand up comedian, organise activist marches, or anything you want really.
